Fluid-interface upgrades: what changes, side by side

Nearly every recommendation in this PR is about motion and latency, which a static screenshot can't show. Each pair below is a working simulation — the “Proposed” cards run the actual physics from the plan (critically-damped springs, exponential momentum decay, grab-to-interrupt). Visual design of the site is intentionally unchanged.

Ranks refer to research/apple-design-fluid-interactions.md. This page honors prefers-reduced-motion: with it enabled, “Proposed” demos fall back to fades and instant states — exactly the behavior the plan specifies (R11).

Rank 1 · R1 — Type-to-render latency

Today every keystroke waits a fixed 300 ms debounce and flashes a spinner even when the render takes 25 ms. Proposed: the debounce adapts to the last measured render time, and the spinner only appears for genuinely slow renders. Press the button in each card and watch the diagram respond.

Rank 4 · R3 — Momentum panning with grab-to-interrupt

Today a drag-pan tracks the pointer 1:1 but stops dead the instant you let go. Proposed: the canvas inherits your release velocity and coasts with scroll-like deceleration — and touching it mid-coast grabs it exactly where it is. Flick each canvas.

Rank 5 · R2 — Smooth, interruptible zoom

Today each zoom step lands as an instant ×1.25 jump (and relayouts the whole SVG). Proposed: one retargetable spring drives the scale — mash + three times fast and it glides to the final target with no jumps, starting every retarget from the live on-screen value.

On the public site's comparisons page, clicking a render pair opens the inspection dialog with no motion at all — it simply appears. Proposed: the dialog scales up from the panel you clicked and returns into it on close, so the spatial relationship is never lost. Click a thumbnail in each card.

Rank 8 · R12 — Toasts stop racing the reader

Editor toasts dismiss on a fixed 2.5 s timer even while you're reading them. Proposed: hovering (or focusing) the toast pauses the timer; it resumes when you leave. Trigger the toast, then hold your pointer over it.

Rank 10 · R6 — Popovers exit the way they entered

Editor menus animate in from their trigger but currently vanish instantly on close — an asymmetry the eye registers as unfinished. Proposed: a 90 ms reverse along the same path, faster than the 160 ms entrance so dismissal still feels immediate. Open and close each menu.

Currententer 160 ms · exit instant

Proposedenter 160 ms · exit 90 ms, same path
symmetric

Rank 11 · R8 — Mobile panel switch: hard cut → soft cut

On phones, switching Source/Preview flips the entire viewport with display:none — a jarring hard cut. Proposed: the incoming panel fades in over 120 ms. Toggle the segmented control.

In the plan, not demoable on this page

  • Rank 2 · R11Curated reduced-motion. Both stylesheets currently zero all animation to 0.01 ms under prefers-reduced-motion, which also kills comprehension-aiding fades and theme-change easing. The plan restores short, transform-free fades — this very page behaves that way.
  • Rank 3 · F1Motion utility. The ~60-line vanilla spring/decay/velocity helper. The Proposed cards above literally run it — it is the prototype.
  • Rank 6 · R10Transparency & contrast fallbacks. prefers-reduced-transparency and prefers-contrast are unhandled today on the frosted shortcuts dialog and lightbox backdrop; the plan adds solid equivalents. No visual change unless the OS signal is set.
  • Rank 9 · R5Two-finger pinch zoom. Touch users currently have no pinch at all in the preview. Needs a touch device to demonstrate; behavior = the R2 spring driven by two pointers.
  • Rank 12 · R15Design-page specimens. The shipped spring values and rulings get added to /about/design and DESIGN.md so future contributors inherit them.
