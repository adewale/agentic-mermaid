# Changelog

This changelog tracks user-facing changes for **Agentic Mermaid**, a fork of `lukilabs/beautiful-mermaid` maintained in the `adewale/beautiful-mermaid` repo and published as the `agentic-mermaid` npm package. Upstream-focused PR branches keep their own minimal histories.

## Unreleased

### Added
- **Port ranking** (issue #26 WS3, completing the yFiles cost model): a shape side carrying exactly one line uses its canonical port — for diamonds, the exact vertex; when no straight vertex lane exists, a single proof-gated Z from the vertex into the target's port outranks a straight lane floating on the facet (lines emit from points, upgraded back to straight by the fixed-point loop when obstacles move). Sides carrying several lines spread along the facet/flat region; fan-ins still merge at the target port; sharper shapes win conflicts and the source wins ties. Verified across all four directions, bi-directional pairs, and diamond-to-diamond chains by tests and the rubric battery. An unlabeled reciprocal pair (A→B with B→A) renders as **two equal parallel lines** straddling the shared centerline at `center ± 6` (primary low, feedback high) — equal deviation gives equal lengths on diamond facets, the way dot offsets reciprocal splines symmetrically about the spine; both pair members enroll in the fixed-point repair pool even when already straight, and any straightening that won on a non-preferred lane (an *upgradeable* success) is re-proved each round so the symmetric lane wins once the partner's geometry settles. Port-only shapes (circles) keep both lines on the exact port. A blocked vertex emit now prefers a **1-bend hook** into the target's *facing* cross-side port (the box's top/bottom in horizontal flow — the IBM flowcharting-manual entry) over the 2-bend Z into the flow-side port; an available fan-in merge at the flow-side port (shared arrowhead) outranks the hook, and a stub shorter than 8px keeps the Z. **Port-lane alignment** (`alignPortLanes`): a "straight but off both midpoints" edge — floating through the sliver where two side-spans overlap because ELK's balanced placement aligned the node with neither neighbor — is now fixed at the placement stage: one endpoint node slides along the cross axis so the two midpoint ports share a lane (port-aware coordinate assignment, Rüegg et al. GD'15), making the edge straight AND port-exact with no routing trade. Slides are proof-gated (shared lane clear of intermediate nodes; sibling edges bent with a perpendicular segment to absorb the shift, labels following translated runs; no node/corridor/label-pill collisions; no group members; aligned endpoints frozen against later re-breaking). The pass covers the whole PORT_EXACT shape catalog — circles, stadiums, hexagons, cylinders, pseudostates, and diamonds slide too (for port-only shapes this converts a forced 2-bend Z into a port-to-port straight; a source diamond with a fan-out never aligns, its vertex has capacity 1; stretched perpendicular segments are proved node-free across their swept corridor) — and an explained detour whose entry lands off-port now seeks a port-exact Z, so the edge left behind by an alignment still converges at the shared entry port. Docs-corpus effect: straight edges 97→101, multi-bend routes 7→5, port-exact endpoints 76.0%→78.2%. **Active fan-in centering**: an unlabeled equal-rank peer fan-in (≥2 distinct unlabeled forward sources stacked in one layer) now snaps the hub to the exact cross-axis barycenter of its sources — correcting ELK's ~20px Brandes–Köpf placement drift — so the incoming edges converge **mirror-symmetric** at the hub's single exact port instead of the old one-straight/one-Z asymmetry. Proof-gated (no node/corridor/label-pill collision; hub not in a group, not also emitting a forward edge; ELK-gridded large fan-ins correctly out of scope). Exact symmetry (0.00px barycenter error, ≤1.5px clipping floor on curved shapes) with zero hard-metric regressions; the trade is +2 bends per fan-in. The L–V contact-sheet scenarios now demonstrate the symmetric merge across every shape. **Diamond facet-mid ports (the 8-port model)**: diamonds gain four extra designated ports at their facet midpoints (NE/SE/SW/NW, all on the outline; `diamondFacetPorts`, recognized by `portAt`, certificates widen to `AnyPort`). Three uses: a diamond fan-out of two forward edges attaches them at the NE/SE facet-mids and slides the targets onto those lanes for **port-to-port straight** edges (E; the vertical case F correctly bails when the targets are too wide to align); a diamond↔diamond reciprocal pair attaches at the nearest facing facet-mids — two parallel lines *between* the diamonds, never through them (G); and a side input below a diamond whose facing port is claimed enters the **S vertex** instead of floating on the facet (K). `shapePorts` stays four-cardinal for every shape and `portAt` is byte-identical for non-diamonds, so this is strictly additive. Heuristic tracker (`bun run track`): 6 improvements, 0 regressions, 0 hard violations across 51 examples. The canonical scenarios are pinned as a **contact sheet** (`bun run contact:sheet` renders lettered scenarios A–S from `eval/visual-rubric/scenarios.ts`; `contact-sheet.test.ts` snapshot-pins each scenario's geometry and asserts zero hard rubric metrics, making the sheet a CI visual-regression gate, now A–V). **Parallelogram shapes** `[/text/]` and `[\text\]` (Mermaid lean_right/lean_left — the ISO 5807 input/output symbol) now parse, render, serialize, and round-trip correctly (previously misparsed as trapezoid/rectangle), and the whole slanted family — parallelograms, trapezoids, the asymmetric flag — joined the PORT_EXACT catalog: exact ports at the true side midpoints (slant midpoints / flag point), flat-side attachment spans, convex-polygon clipping, exact rubric outline oracles, randomized property coverage, and contact-sheet scenarios T–V. `verifyMermaid` gains the Tier-3 `DECISION_BRANCH_UNLABELED` lint (ISO 5807 10.3.1.2 / ANSI X3.5 4.10.2: every exit of a multi-exit decision shall be labeled with its condition value).
- **Deterministic layout-quality rubric + visual testing harness** (`docs/design/layout-rubric.md`): `src/layout-rubric.ts` computes the literature's validated graph-drawing aesthetics from final geometry — seven HARD metrics that must always be zero (endpoints on the rendered shape outline via per-shape closed-form oracles, no diagonal segments, no unexplained bends, no hitches, no node overlaps, labels on their own route, no edge-through-node occlusion deeper than stroke width) plus reported soft metrics (crossings, bends, port-endpoint rates) — citing Purchase 1997/2002, Ware et al. 2002, Kakoulis–Tollis, and the Tamassia orthogonal tradition. `bun run rubric:visual` renders a ~120-fixture simple battery (every routing pattern × direction × shape) and a complicated set into HTML galleries with per-diagram scores and exits nonzero on violations; the same gates run in CI (`layout-rubric.test.ts`) together with fast-check property oracles that mathematically check ports, outlines, and hitches over randomized diagrams. The competitive analysis found no tool in this space gates layout quality with computable metrics (Graphviz: textual regression; Mermaid: pixel-diff; D2: goldens accepted by eye). The property oracles immediately found and forced fixes for: missing shape clipping beyond diamonds (circle/stadium/hexagon/cylinder outlines), the layer-alignment pass snapping nodes onto routed corridors (now occlusion-safe), duplicate edges collapsing onto one path, ELK's degenerate zero-net spike points, a rare 45° feedback-port join (now orthogonalized), flat-side attachment for hexagon/stadium/cylinder (yFiles overflow-candidate pattern), and two ELK internal-crash triggers (now a graceful option-degradation ladder).
- **Route contracts: principled routing without hitches** (issue #25, `docs/design/route-contracts.md`): the flowchart/state layout pipeline now classifies every edge by semantic role (`primary-forward`, `feedback`, `self-loop`, `container`, `cross-hierarchy`), removes duplicate/collinear route points, and collapses a primary-forward dogleg to a straight lane only when a direct-lane proof shows nothing (node, edge label, parallel-channel overlap, attachment-span limit) blocks it — diamonds re-anchor by ray-polygon intersection, labels move onto the straightened segment and count as obstacles for other lanes. Every edge carries a `RouteCertificate` (`edge.routeCertificate` on the positioned graph) recording its class, bend count, and either the straight-lane proof or the concrete blockers (`explained-detour`). Feedback edges route **around the nodes through outer channels** (`elk.layered.feedbackEdges`, the option issue #25 §10 specified) — so the forward lane of a reciprocal pair is straight directly out of ELK and a labeled retry edge renders as the classic loop with its label ON the loop (reserved space, dot's virtual-node doctrine; the earlier offset-pill placement violated the label-to-edge unambiguity criterion and is gone). The pass tightens ELK's loops (exit via the decision diamond's channel-facing facet near the vertex — its "bottom port" — instead of wrapping around the forward side), collapses *unlabeled* feedback onto a parallel straight back-arrow when provably clear, and enforces that no repair ever increases edge crossings. Diamond lanes may now attach anywhere on a facet outside a 10px vertex margin. Blocked feedback stays a `feedback-detour` with named blockers. **Semantic ports** (issue #26 WS3): every shape exposes four canonical connection points at its bbox side midpoints (`shapePorts()` — diamond vertices, rectangle side midpoints, circle/stadium/hexagon/cylinder boundary extremes are the same four points); straightening prefers port lanes (static glue) before floating attachment (dynamic glue), loops exit the diamond's exact S/N vertex and enter the target's exact side midpoint, same-target edges may merge at the shared port, port-only shapes (circles, stadiums, hexagons, cylinders, state pseudostates) join the straightenable set, and certificates record `sourcePort`/`targetPort`. Container edges run border to border: a SEPARATE-hierarchy bug that left a container-to-container edge floating in the diagram margin (caught by the new tripwire on the docs corpus) is repaired by the same proof-carrying straightener with the container rect standing in as a rectangle. The fan-out/fan-in bundler gained the first slice of the bundle contract: rebuilt trunk paths are proved clear of every non-endpoint node, and a blocked member falls out of the bundle instead of routing through a box. The MFA/login regression now renders every clear-lane edge straight in SVG/PNG; corpus comparison: 0 regressions. `verifyMermaid` gains all six Tier 2 advisory route-contract tripwires (`ROUTE_HITCH`, `ROUTE_UNEXPLAINED_BEND`, `ROUTE_LABEL_ON_SHARED_TRUNK`, `ROUTE_CONTAINER_MISANCHOR`, `ROUTE_SHAPE_MISANCHOR`, `ROUTE_STALE_AFTER_NODE_MOVE`) — zero-noise checks that fire only if a pass mutates geometry after route certification. `layoutMermaid(d, { debug: true })` attaches each edge's `RouteCertificate` to the layout JSON. ASCII placement gained longest-path layering (a fan-in target sits after its deepest parent, so skip-edge joins never make a later parent's edge run backward) and labels prefer the widest fitting segment over the crowded final approach. Scoped mutation lane: `bun run mutation-test:routes`.
- **Source-wrapper fidelity (1C) + announced comment policy (2C)**: the leading source wrapper — YAML frontmatter, `%%{init}%%`/`%%{initialize}%%` directives, `%%` comments before the header — now round-trips **byte-verbatim** through `parseMermaid → serializeMermaid` and through every mutation (`meta.wrapperSource`). Previously, `config:`-nested frontmatter was flattened to top-level keys Mermaid cannot read back (silently killing the author's `config.layout`/`config.look` intent on interop), init directives were duplicated into a synthesized frontmatter block, and leading comments were dropped. Canonical wrapper synthesis is now opt-in (`serializeMermaid(d, { wrapper: 'canonical' })`, `am format --canonical-wrapper`) and emits Mermaid's documented shape: `title`/`displayMode` top-level, everything else nested under `config:`, directives folded when their payload is represented (preserved raw when unparseable). In-body `%%` comments are still canonicalized away by structured bodies, but the loss is now *announced*: parse diffs the comments against the serialized output (`meta.droppedComments`) and verify emits the new Tier 3 `COMMENT_DROPPED` lint (count + lines) — never silent. Wrapper comments and comments in opaque bodies/segments are preserved and never warn. See the wrapper law and 2C comment policy in `docs/contributing/adding-diagram-types.md`.
- **Pie and quadrant structured mutation — typed mutation is now the enforced default for every renderable family** (following the journey/architecture/xychart pilots): the modeled subset of `pie` (header `showData`, `title`, `"label" : value` slices) and `quadrantChart` (title, `x-axis`/`y-axis` near/far labels, `quadrant-1..4` region labels, `<Label>: [x, y]` points) now parses to a typed `PieBody`/`QuadrantBody`, narrows via the new `asPie`/`asQuadrant`, and exposes 7 mutation ops each — pie: `set_title`, `set_show_data`, `add_slice`, `remove_slice`, `rename_slice`, `set_slice_value`, `reorder_slice`; quadrant: `set_title`, `set_axis_labels`, `set_quadrant_label`, `add_point`, `remove_point`, `move_point`, `rename_point` — across the library, CLI (`am mutate`), and MCP Code Mode SDK. Quadrant numbering follows Mermaid core (1=top-right, 2=top-left, 3=bottom-left, 4=bottom-right); coordinates stay in `[0, 1]`. Canonical source re-parses 1:1 under the legacy `parsePieChart`/`parseQuadrantChart` (differential-tested). Unmodeled syntax (accTitle/accDescr, malformed pie entries, quadrant styling `classDef`/`:::`, out-of-range coords, duplicate point labels) still falls back to a lossless opaque body, and the renderer keeps surfacing its loud error. This completes the promotion: **all eleven renderable families now ship structured mutation, and a new doc-sync test (`every registered renderable family ships typed mutation`) fails CI if any registered family lacks mutate/serialize hooks, an ops declaration, or a narrower** — see `docs/contributing/adding-diagram-types.md`. Adds the `SLICE_NOT_FOUND` and `POINT_NOT_FOUND` mutation-error codes.
- **Perceptual-quality coverage for every renderable family** (QUAL-1): `layoutMermaid` now projects a real `RenderedLayout` for class, ER, journey, architecture, xychart, pie, and quadrant (joining flowchart/state/sequence/timeline), so `measureQuality`/`checkQuality` and the `verifyMermaid(...).layout` field carry truthful geometry instead of an empty layout. Each adapter (`src/agent/family-layouts.ts`) parses the diagram's canonical source via the family's own parser+layouter — the same geometry the SVG renderer draws — so opaque-but-renderable diagrams are still measured and invalid opaque diagrams degrade to an empty layout rather than throwing. Per family: class/ER nodes are entity/class boxes with relation edges; journey tasks are nodes with section-frame groups; architecture services/junctions are nodes with edges and group frames; xychart bars + line-point markers are nodes with the plot-area group; pie slices map to legend-anchored label boxes; quadrant points are nodes with quadrant-region groups. This is a measurement-only change — rendering bytes are unchanged. The layout-compare harness gains a fixture per family and now scores an empty→measured transition as an improvement, not a faithfulness regression.
- **State diagram structured body** (BUILD-19): state diagrams now own a dedicated `StateBody` IR (`body.kind: 'state'`) instead of being projected as flowcharts. The modeled subset — simple states (`state "Label" as id`, `id : Label`), transitions `from --> to [: label]` with `[*]` start/end pseudostates (scoped per composite level), nestable composite blocks `state X { … }`, and `direction` — parses to a typed body, narrows via the new `asState`, and exposes 8 mutation ops (`add_state`, `remove_state`, `rename_state`, `set_state_label`, `add_transition`, `remove_transition`, `set_transition_label`, `make_composite`) across the library, CLI (`am mutate`), and MCP Code Mode SDK. `remove_state` cascades transitions and refuses a non-empty composite; `rename_state` rewrites transitions. Verify keeps full Tier 1 + Tier 2 geometric coverage: the body projects to a `MermaidGraph` via the legacy parser (`stateBodyToGraph`) and runs the identical flowchart `verifyGraph`. Canonical source re-parses 1:1 under the legacy renderer (differential-tested). Unmodeled syntax (`<<fork>>`/`<<choice>>`/`<<join>>`, history states, concurrency `--`, notes, `classDef`/`class`/`:::` styling, bare `stateId` lines, hyphenated composite ids) falls back to a lossless opaque body. Corpus round-trip for state jumped 5% → 100%. Adds the `STATE_NOT_FOUND`, `TRANSITION_NOT_FOUND`, and `DUPLICATE_STATE` mutation-error codes.
- **Segment-preserving structured sequence body** (BUILD-18): a sequence diagram with `Note`/`alt`/`opt`/`loop`/`par`/`critical`/`break`/`rect`/`activate`/`autonumber`/`title` no longer goes whole-body opaque. The `SequenceBody` now carries an ordered `statements: SequenceStatement[]` list (`participant`/`message` refs into the existing `participants`/`messages` arrays, plus `opaque-block` segments holding unmodeled lines VERBATIM). Such diagrams narrow via `asSequence` and keep all 5 mutation ops live: `add_message` appends after the last statement, `add_participant` inserts after the last participant declaration, and `remove_message`/`set_message_text` indexes address only TOP-LEVEL messages — messages inside an `alt`/`loop` block are part of the verbatim segment and are never touched. Block constructs are captured start→matching-`end` as one nesting-tracked opaque-block. Only un-segmentable input (a stray `end`, an unclosed block) still falls back to a lossless whole-body opaque body. The round-trip stays verbatim-lossless throughout (proven by fast-check properties and the 132-sample MermaidSeqBench + mermaid-docs corpus gates, all still 100% lossless). This ends the all-or-nothing opaque cliff for sequence; class/ER/timeline segment-preservation is follow-up work.
- **Quadrant chart family** (`quadrantChart`, BUILD-11): renders Mermaid quadrant charts to SVG/PNG/ASCII. Supports the `quadrantChart` header, optional `title`, `x-axis <left> --> <right>` / `y-axis <bottom> --> <top>` axis labels (far side optional), four `quadrant-1..quadrant-4` region labels (Mermaid numbering: 1=top-right, 2=top-left, 3=bottom-left, 4=bottom-right), and `<Label>: [x, y]` points with coordinates in `[0, 1]`. SVG draws a square plot split into four theme-tinted quadrants with points as accent circles; ASCII draws a bordered grid with a coordinate legend. Quadrant is source-level (parse/render/verify/round-trip; no typed mutation). Malformed lines — out-of-range/non-numeric coordinates, missing brackets, duplicate point labels, and unsupported styling (`classDef`, `:::`) — error loudly rather than being silently dropped.
- **XY chart structured mutation** (BUILD-16, following the BUILD-15 journey and BUILD-17 architecture pilots): the modeled subset of `xychart-beta` (bare title, named/categorical/range x-axis, named/range y-axis, and `bar`/`line` series with optional names and finite values) now parses to a typed `XyChartBody`, narrows via `asXyChart`, and exposes 8 mutation ops (`set_title`, `set_x_axis`, `set_y_axis`, `add_series`, `remove_series`, `set_series_values`, `set_series_name`, `reorder_series`) across the library, CLI (`am mutate`), and MCP Code Mode SDK. Canonical number format is `String(n)` (finite values only), proven to re-parse identically under the legacy renderer's `parseXYChart`. Unmodeled syntax (quoted text, multi-statement `;` lines, accTitle/accDescr, the `horizontal`-only/other header suffixes beyond orientation, `curve basis`) still falls back to a lossless opaque body. Adds the `SERIES_NOT_FOUND` mutation-error code.
- **Architecture structured mutation** (BUILD-17, following the BUILD-15 journey pilot): the modeled subset of `architecture-beta` (groups/services/junctions and anchored `id:SIDE arrow SIDE:id` edges) now parses to a typed `ArchitectureBody`, narrows via `asArchitecture`, and exposes 10 mutation ops (`add_service`, `remove_service`, `rename_service`, `set_service_label`, `set_service_icon`, `move_service`, `add_group`, `remove_group`, `add_edge`, `remove_edge`) across the library, CLI (`am mutate`), and MCP Code Mode SDK. `remove_service` cascades its edges; `rename_service` keeps edges anchored; `remove_group` refuses a non-empty group. Unmodeled syntax (the `{group}` boundary modifier, accTitle/accDescr, header suffixes) still falls back to a lossless opaque body.
- **Journey structured mutation** (BUILD-15, the pilot promotion of a source-level family): simple journeys (title/sections/`task: score: actors`) now parse to a typed `JourneyBody`, narrow via `asJourney`, and expose 10 mutation ops (`set_title`, `add_section`, `remove_section`, `set_section_label`, `add_task`, `remove_task`, `set_task_text`, `set_task_score`, `set_task_actors`, `rename_actor`) across the library, CLI (`am mutate`), and MCP Code Mode SDK. Unmodeled journey syntax (accTitle/accDescr, out-of-range scores, header suffixes) still falls back to a lossless opaque body.
- Family-plugin dispatch consolidation (BUILD-3): parse, serialize, and mutate for ALL families — including flowchart/state — now route through the `FamilyPlugin` registry; adding a structured family is one body module plus one registration. Flowchart and state register as two plugins sharing one implementation over the legacy graph body, with the serialized header bound per kind; dispatch is by diagram kind, the plugin contract gained `canonicalSource`/multi-error parse and an optional `buildSourceMap` hook. Mutation now rebuilds `canonicalSource` uniformly, so mutated sequence/flowchart/state diagrams no longer carry stale source.
- **Pie chart family** (`pie`): renders Mermaid pie charts to SVG/PNG/ASCII. Supports the `pie` header with optional `showData` and `title`, plus `"label" : value` entries (positive numbers). SVG draws clockwise slices with a theme-derived palette and a percentage legend; ASCII draws a proportional bar list. Pie is source-level (parse/render/verify/round-trip; no typed mutation). Malformed entries error loudly rather than being silently dropped. Adds the first slice of BUILD-5; see `eval/family-usage/` for the family-usage evidence step.
- Layout before/after comparison harness (`eval/layout-compare/run.ts`): snapshots the docs corpus + targeted fixtures (SVG, ASCII, perceptual metrics) per git state and emits a side-by-side HTML report with metric deltas and a regression exit code. Geometry tests now also pin subgraph `direction` support — honored even when an inner node links outward, which Mermaid itself does not solve (mermaid-js#2509).

### Changed
- **Breaking (unreleased agent surface, BUILD-19): flowchart ops no longer apply to state diagrams.** `asFlowchart` now returns `null` on a state diagram (its body kind is `'state'`, not `'flowchart'`); narrow with `asState` and use the state-shaped ops instead. The CLI `MUTATION_OPS_BY_FAMILY.state` list changed from the 6 flowchart ops to the 8 new state ops, and `mutateAny` narrows `asState` before `asFlowchart`. Every agent surface that said "state shares the flowchart body / narrow via asFlowchart" is updated to the new truth.
- SVG style customization is now role-based and diagram-family aware; removed flat render style aliases are intentionally ignored.
- Showcase and editor docs now point users to live examples and presets.
- Fork docs and deploy script now treat GitHub Pages as the fork-owned site and avoid the upstream-owned Craft/Cloudflare deployment target.
- Live editor now starts blank by default, uses salmon as the default theme, uses a larger grouped Examples palette, and includes Copy SVG alongside Save SVG/PNG export.
- Homepage deployment now builds the full sample gallery, defaults to salmon, and removes text that implied Craft affiliation.
- Editor example presets now preserve the currently selected theme instead of forcing Default or Solarized Light.
- Live editor now offers a persistent Examples sidebar and a blank-state “Load an example” CTA.
- Editor controls, empty state, sidebar, and dropdowns received polish for concentric radii, tactile press states, smoother icon transitions, tabular numbers, and cleaner text wrapping.
- Homepage and editor typography now use Atkinson Hyperlegible for a more distinctive, readable UI face.
- Homepage rendering yields between sample batches to keep the page responsive while the full gallery renders.
- Editor empty state now includes quick starter chips for Flowchart, Sequence, and Role styled examples.
- Example rows now include compact diagram-family glyphs for faster scanning.
- Agent-facing Code Mode examples are executable JavaScript snippets and the stored eval now checks ordered verify inspection before serialization.
- Journey and xychart are kept source-level-only in the agent surface; no structured mutation path is exposed even though parser/render dependencies may exist internally.
- Agent guidance now distinguishes new-diagram source authoring from existing-diagram structured mutation; Code Mode is positioned as a structured-edit channel rather than mandatory diagram creation.
- `am capabilities --json` now reports `families[].editPolicy` (`structured-when-narrowed` or `source-level-only`) in addition to `mutationOps`, so agents can route edits without trial-and-error.
- Quality docs now explicitly state that Agentic Mermaid is not Mermaid visual parity: `verify.ok` is structural, while layout quality needs metrics, geometry assertions, screenshots, or rendered artifacts.
- Agent-usage evals now include a committed failure corpus of captured bad-agent paths and curated executable regressions for markdown-only answers, regenerated Mermaid, CLI advice, serialize-without-verify, ignored verify results, and opaque mutation attempts.
- `agentic-mermaid-mcp` now supports HTTP/SSE transport (`--transport http`) in addition to stdio, with managed PNG file/URL artifacts for clients that should not receive large base64 payloads.
- ASCII core hardening (audit follow-up): the widest-segment fallback in `edge-routing.ts` label placement now breaks width ties on a deterministic secondary key (start x, then y) instead of relying on `Array.prototype.sort` stability, so label placement is reproducible across JS engines; verified byte-identical on the layout-compare corpus (261/261 unchanged). (The two index-ordered sorts need no tie-break — segment indexes are unique, a fact the first mutation-testing run proved.) The two confusable subgraph field pairs on `AsciiEdge` (`fromSubgraph`/`toSubgraph` for attachment-point selection vs `attachFromSubgraph`/`attachToSubgraph` for container-border clipping) now carry doc comments naming their consumers, and the fan-in exclusion (self-loops, 2-cycles) and trunk-sharing skip logic in `grid.ts` carry "why" comments with upstream issue references. New `ascii-layout-gaps.test.ts` pins nested subgraph containers, multi-level direction overrides, and unlabeled fan-out trunk sharing. Mutation testing is now scoped to the ASCII core (`docs/mutation-testing.md`).

### Fixed
- ASCII renderer: labelled top-down fan-outs (e.g. `Src -->|left*| Left`, `Src -->|center*| Center`, `Src -->|right*| Right` from one source) now share a single trunk that forks with `┬` tee junctions, and each label sits on its own vertical drop segment — instead of one sibling taking an L-shaped detour with its label stranded on a horizontal run (`─center*─`) and stray corners (upstream lukilabs#111, PR #113). Driven by deterministic FIFO tie-breaking in the A* pathfinder plus label placement that prefers the per-sibling vertical drop in TD; unlabelled fan-outs were already handled by edge-bundling. Pinned by `td_fanout_labeled` golden and charset-independent invariants in `ascii-fanout-trunk-labeled.test.ts`; the LR box-start repro stays byte-identical.
- ASCII renderer: an edge whose endpoint is a subgraph id (e.g. `Start --> Pipeline` where `Pipeline` is a subgraph) no longer renders a phantom duplicate node box. The edge now attaches to the subgraph container — the visible terminal is clipped to the container's border with the arrowhead drawn on the border — matching the SVG/ELK hierarchical-port behavior and Mermaid's handling of the #2509 case. Edge semantics are preserved (no rerouting to an arbitrary inner member node).
- ASCII box-start connectors (`├ ┤ ┬ ┴`) now sit flush on the source node's border instead of floating in whitespace when a sibling edge's label widens the grid column (upstream lukilabs#112 class); the gap is filled with style-matching line characters.
- ASCII fan-in layouts: roots feeding the same target are grouped contiguously and each fan-in target aligns under its own root group (upstream lukilabs#69), so trunk rows of different groups no longer collide into ambiguous `┼` crossings. Self-loops and 2-cycle toggles are excluded so state-machine layouts are unaffected.
- ER cardinality tokens now match Mermaid's lexer on both sides (`||`, `|o`, `o|`, `}o`, `o{`, `}|`, `|{`): left-side `}o` (used by the mermaid-docs corpus as `}o..o{`) previously failed the sort-based normalization and the whole relationship — including its entities — was silently dropped, rendering an empty diagram. Non-Mermaid forms (`{o`, `o}`, `|}`, `{|`) now raise a clear parse error instead of rendering. The render-path parser and the agent ER body parser now agree.
- Showcase and editor theme switching no longer flashes the previous theme's diagram background (white, when leaving a light theme): existing SVG CSS variables are patched instantly, switching back to Default restores each sample's captured original style, and rapid theme switches can no longer interleave stale re-renders.
- TypeScript CI failures in journey style padding and optional node corner-radius resolution.
- Editor export actions are disabled until a diagram exists and parser errors now include recovery-oriented copy.
- Editor menus, sidebar, and theme controls now close with Escape and expose stronger ARIA/focus states.
- Removed layout-property sidebar animation in favor of opacity/transform-based motion.
- CLI/docs drift for `am describe`: the command now emits prose or AX-tree JSON and is covered by e2e tests.
- Feedback-loop flowcharts now preserve the primary source order more reliably instead of ranking decision nodes before their predecessors.
- Acyclic fan-in/fan-out flowcharts now use source-aware model ordering so declared-direction edges do not accidentally route backward; layout-quality heuristics cover direction progress, edge-vs-node collisions, self-loop clearance, and feedback-process cleanliness.
- Tier 3 lint warnings now flag `DUPLICATE_EDGE` and `UNREACHABLE_NODE` in flowchart/state verification without changing `verify.ok`.
